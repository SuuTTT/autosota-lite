Abstract* Ready
Transformer-based multivariate forecasters apply full cross-channel self-attention, which dilutes useful signal when only a structured subset of channel pairs carry meaningful dependencies. We propose **multi-relational masked attention (MRMA), a backbone-agnostic mechanism**: three complementary relation graphs — partial-correlation, Pearson correlation, and a minimum-description-length (MDL)-guided lead-lag community partition — are precomputed from the training split and used as per-head binary masks, specialising each attention head in one relation type. The lead-lag communities are discovered by approximately minimising **2D structural entropy**, a coding-theoretic criterion grounded in MDL theory; unlike modularity-based methods, the structural-entropy objective has no resolution-limit artefacts. The masks are fixed buffers computed once from training data and add **zero parameters**; the same graphs drop unchanged into **three backbones** — iTransformer (yielding MultiRel, our primary instantiation), CrossFormer, and a GRU-based state-space forecaster. On all four standard PEMS traffic benchmarks (PEMS03/04/07/08) under the canonical Time-Series-Library protocol, MultiRel outperforms iTransformer in **all 16 dataset-horizon cells** with gains that grow at longer horizons (**up to 21.5%** at pred-96 on PEMS04); the masked CrossFormer improves its backbone in **12 of 16 cells**; and the three instantiations together attain the **best result in 12 of 16 cells against ten baselines**, including the strongest model on the largest network (N=883) at three of four horizons. Ablations show the unstructured all-ones mask is the **weakest variant in all 16 cells** — the structural prior, not sparsity alone, drives the gains. A practical side-effect is **seed reliability**: unlike learned cross-channel attention (CrossFormer, seed spreads up to 60% under the shared protocol), MRMA stays within **3% worst-case seed spread** while beating its backbone in every cell.
